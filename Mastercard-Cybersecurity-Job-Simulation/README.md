## Investigation Process

### 1. Understanding Phishing Threats
**What I Learned:**
- Phishing is pretending to be someone/something to steal information
- Attackers use links and attachments to infect systems
- Successful phishing costs companies millions
- Employee education is the best defense

### 4. Improved Phishing Email

**My Final Version:**

**From:** it.support@mastercard.com  
**To:** employee@email.com  
**Subject:** Password Reset Required For Corporate Account

**Body:**

Hello Josh,

As part of the company security risk assessment, we have identified a potential issue with your corporate account.

It is mandatory to take immediate action to reset your password within 60 minutes from now. It only takes a few seconds to click and reset. You may also experience a temporary lockout of your account if not reset.

[Click here to reset your password](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Phishing)

Ignore if already done. For more details or further processing information, contact the IT Support Team.

Regards,
Mastercard IT Support.

## Why This Email is More Believable

| Improvement | Why It Works |
|-------------|--------------|
| **Legitimate Sender** | it.support@mastercard.com looks official |
| **Personalization** | Used "Josh" instead of generic greeting |
| **Correct Grammar** | Proper capitalization and spelling |
| **Real Context** | Security risk assessment sounds legitimate |
| **Clear Instructions** | Simple, easy to follow |
| **False Reassurance** | "Ignore if already done" lowers suspicion |
| **Professional Closing** | "Mastercard IT Support" looks official |
| **Masked Link** | Link hidden behind text, looks trustworthy |

## Threat Briefing

### What Makes This Phishing Email Effective:

1. **Contextual Relevance**
   - References Mastercard's security assessment
   - Relates to corporate accounts
   - Uses company-specific terminology

2. **Psychological Triggers**
   - Creates urgency (60-minute deadline)
   - Threatens consequences (account lockout)
   - Appears helpful (offers support contact)

3. **Professional Appearance**
   - No spelling or grammar errors
   - Clean formatting
   - Proper email structure

4. **Trust Building**
   - Uses "Mastercard" branding
   - Offers contact information
   - Provides false reassurance

## Key Learnings

### 1. **Phishing Tactics**
- Attackers use urgency and fear
- Legitimate-looking emails are most dangerous
- Contextual emails have higher success rates

### 2. **Simulation Design**
- Emails must look real to be effective
- Balance believability with training value
- Results should drive training decisions

### 3. **Security Awareness**
- Education is the best defense
- Regular testing is essential
- Everyone plays a role in security
